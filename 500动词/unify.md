1. The papacy, a potential source of political leadership, made little effort to unify the rival Italian communes. Rather, as temporal governors of the Papal States (the lands located in central Italy), Renaissance popes joined in the game of power politics, often allying with one group of city-states against another. (Fiero, 2011f)

> 教皇，一个潜在的政治领导来源，几乎没有努力统一对立的意大利公社。相反，作为教皇国（位于意大利中部的土地）的世俗统治者，文艺复兴时期的教皇加入了权力政治的游戏，经常与一组城邦结盟对抗另一组。

- papacy: 教皇

- temporal governors: 世俗统治者
- Papal States: 教皇国
- lands located in: 位于...的土地
- central Italy: 意大利中部

- game of power politics: 权力政治的游戏
- often: 经常
- allying with: 与...结盟
- one group: 一组
- city-states: 城邦
- against another: 对抗另一组

2. Despite Charlemagne's attempt to unify Europe, there was no central political authority, for Europe was still divided into small units ruled by powerful families. (Davies et al., 2015)

> 尽管查理曼试图统一欧洲，但没有中央政治权威，因为欧洲仍然被分割成由强大家族统治的小单位。

- unify: 统一
- despite: 尽管

- Europe: 欧洲

> 然而，19世纪末的响亮和沙文主义的民族主义并没有统一人民，而是分裂了他们，因为新的民族国家在1871年后陷入了激烈的竞争。
