## 影响；塑造

1. Despite the best efforts of contemporary art historians, many assessments of art today are deeply informed by a notion of beauty rooted in Greek Classicism. (Davies et al., 2015)

> 尽管当代艺术史学家做出了最大努力，但今天对艺术的许多评估都深深受到根植于希腊古典主义的美学观念的影响。

- inform: 影响，塑造
- despite: 尽管
- best efforts: 最大努力
- contemporary art historians: 当代艺术史学家
- assessments: 评估
- deeply informed: 深深受到...影响
- notion: 观念
- beauty: 美学
- rooted in: 根植于
- Greek Classicism: 希腊古典主义

2. Herbert Blumer's 1930s study of the impact of cinema on the audience was an early attempt to allow people themselves to inform sociological understandings of media influence. (Giddens, 2009)

> 赫伯特·布鲁默在20世纪30年代对电影对观众影响的研究是一个早期尝试，让人们自己来影响对媒体影响的社会学理解。

- inform: 影响，塑造
- study: 研究
- impact: 影响
- cinema: 电影
- audience: 观众
- early attempt: 早期尝试
- allow: 允许
- sociological understandings: 社会学理解
- media influence: 媒体影响

3. According to the dualistic model, the human body operates much like a computer, with the immaterial mind (the software) "informing" the physical components of the body (the hardware). (Fiero, 2011d)

> 根据二元论模型，人体运作很像计算机，非物质的心灵（软件）"影响"身体的物理组成部分（硬件）。

- inform: 影响
- according to: 根据
- dualistic model: 二元论模型
- human body: 人体
- operates: 运作
- computer: 计算机
- immaterial mind: 非物质的心灵
- software: 软件
- physical components: 物理组成部分
- hardware: 硬件

4. The DTM (Demographic Transition Model) has been perhaps the most influential perspective on long-term population trends ever devised and it continues to inform research in the field of demography.

> 人口转型模型（DTM）可能是迄今为止设计出的关于长期人口趋势的最有影响力的视角，它继续影响着人口学领域的研究。

- inform: 影响
- DTM: 人口转型模型
- Demographic Transition Model: 人口转型模型
- influential: 有影响力的
- perspective: 视角
- long-term population trends: 长期人口趋势
- devised: 设计出的
- continues: 继续
- research: 研究
- field: 领域
- demography: 人口学

2. Fortunately, there are nonaggressive ways to express our feelings and to inform others how their behavior affects us. (Myers, 2012)

3. When King Louis XVI was informed of the fall of the Bastille by the duc de La Rochefoucauld-Liancourt, he exclaimed, "Why, this is a revolt." "No, Sire," replied the duke, "it is a revolution."
