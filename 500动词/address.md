## 处理

1. As communities flourished, they grew into city-states with distinct patterns of social organization to address the problems of urban life. (Davies et al., 2015)

> 随着社区繁荣发展，它们成长为具有独特社会组织模式的城市国家，以处理城市生活的问题。

- address: 处理
- as communities flourished: 随着社区繁荣发展
- grew into: 成长为
- city-states: 城市国家
- distinct patterns: 独特模式
- social organization: 社会组织
- problems of urban life: 城市生活的问题

2. African poets addressed the fragility of human life, celebrated the transition from one stage of growth to another, honored the links between the living and the dead, praised heroes and rulers, and recounted the experiences of everyday life. (Fiero, 2011c)

> 非洲诗人处理人类生命的脆弱性，庆祝从一个成长阶段到另一个阶段的转变，尊重生者与死者之间的联系，赞美英雄和统治者，并叙述日常生活的经历。

- address: 处理
- African poets: 非洲诗人
- fragility of human life: 人类生命的脆弱性
- celebrated: 庆祝
- transition: 转变
- from one stage of growth to another: 从一个成长阶段到另一个
- honored: 尊重
- links between the living and the dead: 生者与死者之间的联系
- praised: 赞美
- heroes and rulers: 英雄和统治者
- recounted: 叙述
- experiences of everyday life: 日常生活的经历

3. In order to address these new issues, sociologists have been forced to re-evaluate the utility of the classical perspectives and, where these are found wanting, to develop novel theories of their own. (Giddens, 2009)

> 为了处理这些新问题，社会学家被迫重新评估古典观点的效用，并在发现这些观点不足的地方，发展他们自己的新理论。

- address: 处理
- in order to: 为了
- these new issues: 这些新问题
- sociologists: 社会学家
- have been forced: 被迫
- re-evaluate: 重新评估
- utility: 效用
- classical perspectives: 古典观点
- where these are found wanting: 在发现这些不足的地方
- develop: 发展
- novel theories: 新理论
- of their own: 他们自己的

## 致力于某事

1. Separation of the races by segregated housing, inferior schools, and exclusion from voting and equal employment were only a few of the inequities suffered by this minority in the post-emancipation United States. It was to these issues and to the more general problem of racism that many African-Americans addressed themselves after World War I. (Fiero, 2011f)

> 通过隔离住房、劣等学校和排除投票与平等就业的种族分离只是这个少数群体在后解放时期的美国所遭受的不公正中的少数几个。正是这些问题和更普遍的种族主义问题，许多非裔美国人在第一次世界大战后致力于解决。

- address themselves: 致力于
- separation of the races: 种族分离
- segregated housing: 隔离住房
- inferior schools: 劣等学校
- exclusion from: 排除
- voting and equal employment: 投票与平等就业
- only a few: 只是少数几个
- inequities: 不公正
- suffered by: 遭受
- this minority: 这个少数群体
- post-emancipation United States: 后解放时期的美国
- it was to these issues: 正是这些问题
- more general problem: 更普遍的问题
- racism: 种族主义
- many African-Americans: 许多非裔美国人
- after World War I: 第一次世界大战后

2. Stressing the intellect and self-reliance, Greco-Roman thought did not provide for the emotional needs of the ordinary person. Christianity addressed itself to this defect in the Greco-Roman outlook. (Perry, 2010)

> 强调智力和自力更生，希腊罗马思想没有为普通人的情感需求提供支持。基督教致力于解决希腊罗马观点中的这一缺陷。

- address itself to: 致力于
- stressing: 强调
- intellect: 智力
- self-reliance: 自力更生
- Greco-Roman thought: 希腊罗马思想
- did not provide for: 没有为...提供支持
- emotional needs: 情感需求
- ordinary person: 普通人
- Christianity: 基督教
- defect: 缺陷
- Greco-Roman outlook: 希腊罗马观点

3. It is not from the benevolence of the butcher, the brewer, or the baker that we expect our dinner, but from their regard to their own interest. We address ourselves, not to their humanity but to their self-love, and never talk to them of our own necessities but of their advantages. (Wolff, 2006)

> 我们期望的晚餐不是来自屠夫、酿酒师或面包师的仁慈，而是来自他们对自身利益的关注。我们致力于的，不是他们的人性，而是他们的自爱，从不向他们谈论我们自己的必需品，而是谈论他们的优势。

- address ourselves: 致力于
- not from: 不是来自
- benevolence: 仁慈
- butcher: 屠夫
- brewer: 酿酒师
- baker: 面包师
- expect our dinner: 期望我们的晚餐
- but from: 而是来自
- regard to their own interest: 对自身利益的关注
- not to their humanity: 不是他们的人性
- but to their self-love: 而是他们的自爱
- never talk to them: 从不向他们谈论
- our own necessities: 我们自己的必需品
- but of their advantages: 而是他们的优势

4. John Locke addressed himself primarily to the first question in his writings on property: how can an individual form a right to property appropriated from its natural state? (Wolff, 2006)

> 约翰·洛克在他的财产著作中主要致力于第一个问题：个人如何形成从自然状态中占有的财产权？

- address himself to: 致力于
- John Locke: 约翰·洛克
- primarily: 主要地
- first question: 第一个问题
- writings on property: 关于财产的著作
- how can: 如何能
- individual: 个人
- form a right: 形成权利
- property: 财产
- appropriated from: 从...占有
- natural state: 自然状态
