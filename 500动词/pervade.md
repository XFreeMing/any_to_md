1. Uncertainty also pervaded the cultural and intellectual achievements of the interwar years. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 不确定性也弥漫在两次世界大战之间的文化和知识成就中。

- pervade: 弥漫
- uncertainty: 不确定性
- also: 也
- cultural achievements: 文化成就
- intellectual achievements: 知识成就

2. Charles Dickens was one of Britain's greatest novelists. While he realistically portrayed the material, social, and psychological milieu of his time, an element of Romanticism still pervaded his novels. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 查尔斯·狄更斯是英国最伟大的小说家之一。虽然他现实主义地描绘了他那个时代的物质、社会和心理环境，但浪漫主义的元素仍然弥漫在他的小说中。

- pervade: 弥漫
- Charles Dickens: 查尔斯·狄更斯
- one of: 之一
- Britain's greatest novelists: 英国最伟大的小说家
- while: 虽然
- realistically portrayed: 现实主义地描绘
- material milieu: 物质环境
- social milieu: 社会环境
- psychological milieu: 心理环境
- his time: 他的时代
- element of Romanticism: 浪漫主义的元素
- still: 仍然
- novels: 小说

3. The concept suggests that racism pervades all of society's structures in a systematic manner. According to this view, institutions such as the police, the health service and the education system all promote policies that favour certain groups, while discriminating against others. (Giddens, 2009)

> 这个概念表明，种族主义以系统的方式弥漫在社会所有结构中。根据这种观点，警察、卫生服务和教育系统等机构都推行有利于某些群体而歧视其他群体的政策。

- pervade: 弥漫
- concept: 概念
- suggests: 表明
- racism: 种族主义
- all of society's structures: 社会所有结构
- systematic manner: 系统的方式

- view: 观点
- institutions: 机构
- such as: 例如
- police: 警察
- health service: 卫生服务
- education system: 教育系统
- promote policies: 推行政策
- favour: 有利于

- pervade: 弥漫
