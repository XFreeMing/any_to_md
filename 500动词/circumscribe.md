1. Civil liberties, such as freedom of the press, of speech, of assembly, and of movement, were circumscribed in the name of national security. (Perry, 2010)

> 公民自由，如新闻自由、言论自由、集会自由和行动自由，以国家安全的名义受到限制。

- circumscribe: 限制，约束

- civil liberties: 公民自由

- freedom of the press: 新闻自由

- freedom of speech: 言论自由

- freedom of assembly: 集会自由

- freedom of movement: 行动自由

- in the name of: 以...的名义

- national security: 国家安全

  

2. With this gesture, Duchamp makes an important move in his exploration of the essence of art. A title, which defines a work, circumscribes its meaning, and also serves as a tool for remembering the work, fulfilling a role as important as the artwork itself. (Davies et al., 2015)

> 通过这个姿态，杜尚在探索艺术的本质方面迈出了重要一步。标题定义了作品，限定了其意义，也作为记忆作品的工具，发挥着与艺术品本身同样重要的作用。

- circumscribe: 限定，界定

- gesture: 姿态

- exploration: 探索

- essence: 本质

- defines: 定义

- meaning: 意义

- serves as: 作为

- tool for remembering: 记忆工具

- fulfilling: 履行，发挥

- role: 作用

- artwork: 艺术品

  

3. Although Nebuchadnezzar, "King of Kings" of the biblical Daniel, had boasted that he "caused a mighty wall to circumscribe Babylon . . . so that the enemy who would do evil would not threaten," Cyrus of Persia (r. 559–529 BCE) captured the city in the sixth century. (Kleiner, 2015)

> 尽管《圣经》中但以理书中的"万王之王"尼布甲尼撒曾夸口说他"建造了一座强大的城墙来围绕巴比伦...这样想要作恶的敌人就不会威胁它"，但波斯的居鲁士（公元前559-529年在位）在六世纪攻占了这座城市。

- circumscribe: 围绕，环绕

- boasted: 夸口

- mighty wall: 强大的城墙

- enemy: 敌人

- threaten: 威胁

- captured: 攻占

- century: 世纪

  

4. A plan, essentially a map of a floor, shows the placement of a structure's masses and, therefore, the spaces they circumscribe and enclose. (Davies et al., 2015)

> 平面图本质上是楼层的地图，显示了结构体的位置，因此也显示了它们围绕和围合的空间。

- circumscribe: 围绕，界定

- plan: 平面图

- essentially: 本质上

- map of a floor: 楼层地图

- placement: 位置

- structure's masses: 结构体

- spaces: 空间

- enclose: 围合
