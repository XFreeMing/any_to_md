1. Situated where the Tiber could be readily forded, Rome became a natural crossing point for north-south traffic in western Italy. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 位于台伯河可以轻易涉水而过的地方，罗马成为意大利西部南北交通的自然过境点。

- situate: 位于，坐落

- Tiber: 台伯河

- readily: 轻易地

- forded: 涉水而过

- Rome: 罗马

- natural crossing point: 自然过境点

- north-south traffic: 南北交通

- western Italy: 意大利西部

  

2. Galileo's position was clear: "I hold the sun to be situated motionless in the center of the revolution of the celestial bodies, while the earth rotates on its axis and revolves about the sun." (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 伽利略的立场很明确："我认为太阳位于天体运行的中心，静止不动，而地球在其轴上旋转并围绕太阳运行。"

- situate: 位于

- Galileo: 伽利略

- position: 立场

- clear: 明确的

- sun: 太阳

- motionless: 静止不动

- center: 中心

- revolution: 运行

- celestial bodies: 天体

- earth: 地球

- rotates: 旋转

- axis: 轴

- revolves about: 围绕...运行

  

3. The plan was later modified to situate the new Island Airport on the "Outer Harbour" headland, east of the Islands, with road access available from the Gardiner Expressway south along Leslie Street. ("Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport," 2018)

> 该计划后来被修改，将新岛机场设在"外港"海角，位于群岛的东部，可通过加德纳高速公路向南沿莱斯利街提供道路通道。

- situate: 设置，位于

- plan: 计划

- modified: 修改

- Island Airport: 岛机场

- Outer Harbour: 外港

- headland: 海角

- east of: 东部

- Islands: 群岛

- road access: 道路通道

- available: 可用的

- Gardiner Expressway: 加德纳高速公路

- south along: 向南沿

- Leslie Street: 莱斯利街

  

4. Eighteen centuries before Copernicus, the Alexandrian astronomer Aristarchus (310–230 b.c.) said that the sun was the center of the universe, that the planets revolved around it, and that the stars were situated at great distances from the earth. (Perry, 2010)

> 在哥白尼之前十八个世纪，亚历山大的天文学家阿里斯塔克（公元前310-230年）说，太阳是宇宙的中心，行星围绕它运行，恒星位于离地球很远的地方。

- situate: 位于

- eighteen centuries: 十八个世纪

- before: 在...之前

- Copernicus: 哥白尼

- Alexandrian astronomer: 亚历山大的天文学家

- Aristarchus: 阿里斯塔克

- sun: 太阳

- center: 中心

- universe: 宇宙

- planets: 行星

- revolved around: 围绕...运行

- stars: 恒星

- great distances: 很远的距离

- earth: 地球
