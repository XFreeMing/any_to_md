- similar age group: 相似年龄组

- together: 一起

- hold: 拥有

- similar rights: 相似的权利

- obligations: 义务

  

2. The mere-exposure effect has "enormous adaptive significance," notes Zajonc (1998). It is a "hardwired" phenomenon that predisposes our attractions and attachments. It helped our ancestors categorize things and people as either familiar and safe or unfamiliar and possibly dangerous. The more two strangers interact, the more attractive they tend to find each other (Reis & others, 2011). (Myers, 2012)

> 仅仅暴露效应具有"巨大的适应意义"，扎荣茨（1998）指出。这是一个"硬连线"的现象，它预先决定了我们的吸引力和依恋。它帮助我们的祖先将事物和人归类为熟悉和安全的，或陌生和可能危险的。两个陌生人互动越多，他们往往越觉得对方有吸引力。

- categorize: 归类

- mere-exposure effect: 仅仅暴露效应

- enormous adaptive significance: 巨大的适应意义

- hardwired: 硬连线的

- phenomenon: 现象

- predisposes: 预先决定

- attractions: 吸引力

- attachments: 依恋

- ancestors: 祖先

- familiar: 熟悉的

- safe: 安全的

- unfamiliar: 陌生的

- possibly dangerous: 可能危险的

- strangers: 陌生人

- interact: 互动

- attractive: 有吸引力的

  

3. Many people accept that they have impairments, but do not wish to be labelled as 'disabled'. In a recent survey of people claiming government benefits for disability, fewer than half chose to define themselves as disabled. Many people rejected the term because they saw their health problems related to illness rather than disability or because they did not think that they were ill enough to be so categorized (Department for Work and Pensions 2002). (Giddens, 2009)

> 许多人接受他们有损伤，但不想被标记为"残疾人"。在最近一项申请政府残疾福利的人的调查中，不到一半的人选择将自己定义为残疾人。许多人拒绝这个术语，因为他们认为他们的健康问题与疾病有关而不是残疾，或者因为他们认为他们病得不够严重，不足以被如此归类。

- categorize: 归类

- accept: 接受

- impairments: 损伤

- wish: 希望

- labelled: 标记

- disabled: 残疾人

- recent survey: 最近的调查

- claiming: 申请

- government benefits: 政府福利

- disability: 残疾

- fewer than half: 不到一半

- chose: 选择

- define themselves: 将自己定义为

- rejected: 拒绝

- term: 术语

- health problems: 健康问题

- related to: 与...相关

- illness: 疾病

- rather than: 而不是

- think: 认为

- ill enough: 病得足够严重

  

4. By the age of 2, children have a partial understanding of what gender is. They know whether they are a boy or a girl, and they can usually categorize others accurately.

> 到2岁时，儿童对性别有部分理解。他们知道自己是男孩还是女孩，通常可以准确地归类他人。

- categorize: 归类

- by the age of: 到...岁时

- children: 儿童

- partial understanding: 部分理解

- gender: 性别

- know: 知道

- whether: 是否

- boy: 男孩

- girl: 女孩

- usually: 通常

- accurately: 准确地

- others: 他人
