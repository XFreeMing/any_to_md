1. Narrative history effectively transmits the knowledge of the past and is the form that best enables students to remember and understand the past. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 叙事历史有效地传递过去的知识，是使学生能够最好地记住和理解过去的形式。

- enables: 使能够
- narrative history: 叙事历史
- effectively transmits: 有效地传递
- knowledge of the past: 过去的知识
- is the form: 是形式
- best: 最好地
- students: 学生
- remember: 记住
- understand: 理解
- the past: 过去

2. The invention of writing enabled records to be kept, and the use of metals marked a new level of human control over the environment and its resources. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 文字的发明使记录得以保存，金属的使用标志着人类对环境及其资源的控制达到了新的水平。

- enabled: 使能够
- invention of writing: 文字的发明
- records: 记录
- to be kept: 得以保存
- use of metals: 金属的使用
- marked: 标志着
- new level: 新水平
- human control: 人类控制
- over: 对
- environment: 环境
- its resources: 其资源

3. As we have seen, early human beings formed small groups that developed a simple culture that enabled them to survive. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 正如我们所看到的，早期人类形成了小群体，这些群体发展出一种简单的文化，使他们能够生存。

- enabled: 使能够
- as we have seen: 正如我们所看到的
- early human beings: 早期人类
- formed: 形成
- small groups: 小群体
- developed: 发展
- simple culture: 简单的文化
- them: 他们
- to survive: 生存

4. Fire also enabled early humans to cook their food, making it taste better, last longer, and in the case of some plants, such as wild grain, easier to chew and digest. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 火也使早期人类能够烹饪他们的食物，使其味道更好，保存更久，对于某些植物，如野生谷物，更容易咀嚼和消化。

- enabled: 使能够
- fire: 火
- also: 也
- early humans: 早期人类
- to cook: 烹饪
- their food: 他们的食物
- making it: 使其
- taste better: 味道更好
- last longer: 保存更久
- in the case of: 对于
- some plants: 某些植物
- such as: 如
- wild grain: 野生谷物
- easier to chew and digest: 更容易咀嚼和消化
