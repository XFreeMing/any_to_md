2. Unlike Berman et al. (1998) who argue that productivity shocks are the primary source of international transmissions of business cycles, Feenstra finds that demand shocks are more important. Feenstra uses monthly government expenditure data in the United States and Mexico to calibrate demand shocks and monthly Solow residual data to calibrate supply shocks. His results indicate that home demand shocks are the most important driver of volatility in the Mexican offshoring sector, while productivity shocks generate much less volatility in employment. (McMillan, 2010)

> 与认为生产力冲击是商业周期国际传播主要来源的伯曼等人（1998）不同，芬斯特拉发现需求冲击更重要。芬斯特拉使用美国和墨西哥的月度政府支出数据来校准需求冲击，使用月度索洛残差数据来校准供给冲击。他的结果表明，国内需求冲击是墨西哥离岸部门波动的最重要驱动因素，而生产力冲击在就业中产生的波动要少得多。

3. First, science is, in part, a human activity. Typically, it includes such activities as observing, collecting and classifying data, setting up and carrying out experiments, calibrating scientific instruments, constructing hypotheses, theories and models, finding evidence, reading a scientific paper and so on.
