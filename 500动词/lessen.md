2. Some companies organize on-the-job training through job-sharing teams. This technique allows skills training and mentoring to take place at the same time as work is getting done: an IT specialist might be paired for several weeks with a company manager in order for each to learn some of the other's skills. This form of training is cost-effective, as it does not significantly lessen working hours and allows all employees involved to broaden their skills base. (Giddens, 2009)
