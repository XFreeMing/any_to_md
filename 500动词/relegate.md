1. Like many ethnic minorities, women have long been relegated to the position of second-class citizens. (Fiero, 2011f)

> 像许多少数民族一样，女性长期以来被降级到二等公民的地位。

- relegate: 降级
- like: 像
- many ethnic minorities: 许多少数民族
- women: 女性
- long been: 长期以来被
- position: 地位
- second-class citizens: 二等公民

2. By the second century, however, men had gained control of church organization and relegated women to secondary roles. Women, as Paul had argued, should be subject to men. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 然而，到了二世纪，男性已经控制了教会组织，并将女性降级到次要角色。正如保罗所论证的，女性应该服从男性。

- relegate: 降级
- by the second century: 到了二世纪
- however: 然而
- men: 男性
- gained control: 控制了
- church organization: 教会组织
- women: 女性
- secondary roles: 次要角色
- as Paul had argued: 正如保罗所论证的
- should be subject to: 应该服从

3. Migrants in search of a better standard of living are often relegated to casual jobs that offer low wages and few career prospects. (Giddens, 2009)

> 寻求更好生活水平的移民经常被降级到提供低工资和很少职业前景的临时工作。

- relegate: 降级
- migrants: 移民
- in search of: 寻求
- better standard of living: 更好的生活水平
- often: 经常
- casual jobs: 临时工作
- offer: 提供
- low wages: 低工资
- few career prospects: 很少职业前景

4. If the guidance of education is not returned to the priests, and if science is not uniformly relegated to a subordinate rank, incalculable evils await us. (Perry, 2010)

> 如果教育的指导不归还给牧师，如果科学不统一地被降级到从属地位，不可估量的邪恶等待着我们。

- relegate: 降级
- if: 如果
- guidance: 指导
- education: 教育
- returned to: 归还给
- priests: 牧师
- science: 科学
- uniformly: 统一地
- subordinate rank: 从属地位
- incalculable: 不可估量的
- evils: 邪恶
- await: 等待
- us: 我们
