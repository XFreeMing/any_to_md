1. After being educated, you cannot regress toward state that is without knowledge. It is an irreversible process. (Myers, 2012)

> 在接受教育后，你无法退回到没有知识的状态。这是一个不可逆转的过程。

- regress: 退回
- after being educated: 在接受教育后
- cannot: 无法
- toward: 朝向
- state: 状态
- without knowledge: 没有知识
- irreversible process: 不可逆转的过程

2. We puzzle at why baseball's rookie of the year often has a more ordinary second year—did he become overconfident? Self-conscious? We forget that exceptional performance tends to regress toward normality. (Myers, 2012)

> 我们困惑于为什么棒球年度新秀往往在第二年表现更普通——他变得过于自信了吗？自我意识过强？我们忘记了卓越的表现往往会回归到正常水平。

- regress: 回归
- puzzle at: 困惑于
- why: 为什么
- baseball's rookie of the year: 棒球年度新秀
- often: 往往
- more ordinary: 更普通的
- second year: 第二年
- become overconfident: 变得过于自信
- self-conscious: 自我意识过强
- forget: 忘记
- exceptional performance: 卓越的表现
- tends to: 往往会
- toward normality: 朝向正常水平

3. Preadolescent children, who seem to experience a deep sadness and anxiety about the future, are usually the most upset. Some may regress to immature behavior, wetting their beds or becoming excessively possessive. Most children, regardless of their age, are angry because of the separation. (Strong, DeVault, & Cohen, 2010)

> 青春期前的儿童，他们似乎对未来感到深深的悲伤和焦虑，通常是最不安的。有些人可能会退回到不成熟的行为，尿床或变得过度占有。大多数儿童，无论年龄如何，都因为分离而愤怒。

- regress: 退回
- preadolescent children: 青春期前的儿童
- seem to experience: 似乎感到
- deep sadness: 深深的悲伤
- anxiety: 焦虑
- about the future: 关于未来
- usually: 通常
- most upset: 最不安的
- some: 有些人
- may: 可能会
- immature behavior: 不成熟的行为
- wetting their beds: 尿床
- becoming excessively possessive: 变得过度占有
- most children: 大多数儿童
- regardless of: 无论
- age: 年龄
- angry: 愤怒
- because of: 因为
- separation: 分离

4. The patient had regressed to a state of childish dependency.

> 病人已经退回到一种幼稚依赖的状态。

- regress: 退回
- patient: 病人
- had: 已经
- state: 状态
- childish dependency: 幼稚依赖
