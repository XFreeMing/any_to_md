## 指定；任命

1. The senate continued to designate the governors of the remaining provinces, but the authority of Augustus enabled him to overrule the senatorial governors and establish a uniform imperial policy. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 元老院继续指定剩余省份的总督，但奥古斯都的权威使他能够推翻元老院的总督并建立统一的帝国政策。

- designate: 指定，任命
- senate: 元老院
- continued: 继续
- governors: 总督
- remaining provinces: 剩余省份
- authority: 权威
- enabled: 使能够
- overrule: 推翻
- senatorial: 元老院的
- establish: 建立
- uniform: 统一的
- imperial policy: 帝国政策

2. She has been designated to take over the position of treasurer. (Longman Dictionary of Contemporary English, 2015)

> 她被指定接任财务主管的职位。

- designate: 指定
- take over: 接任
- position: 职位
- treasurer: 财务主管

3. Today, because we need to distinguish which category a scholar fits into, we designate people as geo-archaeologists, zoo-archaeologists, or palaeo-botanists. And we, still, find archaeologists within departments of classics, medieval history, and anthropology. (Cunliffe, Joyce, & Gosden, 2009)

> 今天，因为我们需要区分学者属于哪个类别，我们将人们指定为地质考古学家、动物考古学家或古植物学家。我们仍然在古典学、中世纪史和人类学系中找到考古学家。

- designate: 指定，分类
- distinguish: 区分
- category: 类别
- scholar: 学者
- fits into: 属于
- geo-archaeologists: 地质考古学家
- zoo-archaeologists: 动物考古学家
- palaeo-botanists: 古植物学家
- archaeologists: 考古学家
- departments: 系
- classics: 古典学
- medieval history: 中世纪史
- anthropology: 人类学

4. Some claimed that the emperor had been designated by his father.

> 有些人声称皇帝是由他的父亲指定的。

- designate: 指定
- claimed: 声称
- emperor: 皇帝
- father: 父亲
