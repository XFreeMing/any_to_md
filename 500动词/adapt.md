3. The design on the back of a mirror created soon after 400 BCE is of a different genre, and shows how the Etruscans adapted Greek traditions to their own ends. Within an undulating wreath of vines stands a winged old man, one foot raised upon a rock. An inscription identifies him as the seer Chalchas, an Etruscan version of the Greek figure known from Homer's Iliad. (Davies et al., 2015)
