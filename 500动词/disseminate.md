1. Christianity spread slowly at first. Although the teachings of early Christianity were disseminated primarily by the preaching of convinced Christians, written materials also appeared.  (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 基督教最初传播缓慢。虽然早期基督教的教义主要通过确信的基督徒的讲道传播，但也出现了书面材料。

- Christianity: 基督教
- spread slowly: 传播缓慢
- at first: 最初
- although: 虽然
- teachings: 教义
- early Christianity: 早期基督教
- primarily: 主要地
- preaching: 讲道
- convinced Christians: 确信的基督徒
- written materials: 书面材料
- appeared: 出现

2. Of greater importance to the work of science, however, was the creation of a scientific method and new learned societies that enabled the new scientists to communicate their ideas to each other and to disseminate them to a wider, literate public. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 然而，对科学工作更重要的是科学方法的创建和新的学术团体的创建，这些使新科学家能够相互交流他们的想法，并将它们传播给更广泛的、有文化的公众。

- greater importance: 更重要
- work of science: 科学工作
- however: 然而
- creation: 创建
- scientific method: 科学方法
- new learned societies: 新的学术团体

3. In fact, there are many connections between the building arts and the elaborate devotional works with exquisite miniatures produced for the personal enjoyment and education of the royal family and for others who were literate and could afford them. These products of French manuscript workshops disseminated the refined taste that made the court art of Paris the standard for all Europe. (Davies et al., 2015)
