## 做出；给予

1. The facts of a case are usually compelling enough that jurors can lay aside their biases and render a fair judgment. (Myers, 2012)

> 案件的事实通常足够有说服力，陪审员可以放下他们的偏见并做出公正的判决。

- render: 做出，给予
- facts: 事实
- case: 案件
- compelling: 有说服力的
- jurors: 陪审员
- lay aside: 放下
- biases: 偏见
- fair judgment: 公正的判决

2. The conquered had to render tribute to the conqueror. (Perry, 2010)

> 被征服者必须向征服者进贡。

- render: 给予，提供
- conquered: 被征服者
- tribute: 贡品
- conqueror: 征服者

3. You might be willing to provide something helpful to others, but do you believe we have an obligation to render assistance to those in need? (Copp, 2011)

> 你可能愿意为他人提供有用的东西，但你是否认为我们有义务向需要帮助的人提供援助？

- render: 提供
- willing: 愿意
- provide: 提供
- helpful: 有用的
- believe: 认为
- obligation: 义务
- assistance: 援助
- those in need: 需要帮助的人

4. It is unlikely that the court will render an opinion before November. (Copp, 2005)

> 法院不太可能在11月之前发表意见。

- render: 发表，给出
- unlikely: 不太可能
- court: 法院
- opinion: 意见
- before: 在...之前

## 以某种方式表达；表现；使成为

1. Sometimes the fit isn't comfortable, but he renders details in such a way as to make every object as concrete as possible in its shape, size, color, and texture. (Davies et al., 2015)

2. Infrared film renders blue skies a deep black.

3. The use of gunpowder and the longbow, which put physical distance between combatants, worked to outmode hand-to-hand combat, thus rendering obsolete the medieval code of chivalry. (Fiero, 2011c)

4. The blow to his head was strong enough to render him unconscious. (Miller & Perlman, 2011)
