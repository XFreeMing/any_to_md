1. Protestantism fragmented into a number of sects, which, though united in their dislike of Catholicism, were themselves divided over the interpretation of the sacraments and religious practices. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 新教分裂成许多教派，这些教派虽然在对天主教的厌恶上团结一致，但它们自己在对圣礼和宗教实践的解释上存在分歧。

- fragment: 分裂
- Protestantism: 新教
- number of sects: 许多教派
- though: 虽然
- united in: 在...上团结一致
- dislike of Catholicism: 对天主教的厌恶
- themselves: 它们自己
- divided over: 在...上存在分歧
- interpretation: 解释
- sacraments: 圣礼
- religious practices: 宗教实践

2. It is generally agreed that the old, traditional working-class communities have tended to become fragmented, or have broken down altogether, with the decline of manufacturing industry. (Giddens, 2009)

> 人们普遍认为，随着制造业的衰落，旧的、传统的工人阶级社区倾向于变得支离破碎，或者完全崩溃。

- fragment: 支离破碎
- generally agreed: 普遍认为
- old, traditional: 旧的、传统的
- working-class communities: 工人阶级社区
- tended to become: 倾向于变得
- broken down altogether: 完全崩溃
- decline: 衰落
- manufacturing industry: 制造业

3. Parsons was the first major social theorist to provide a synthesis of classical social theory, which had fragmented into the traditions represented by Marx, Durkheim, and Weber. (Giddens, 2009)

> 帕森斯是第一个提供古典社会理论综合的主要社会理论家，古典社会理论已经分裂成由马克思、涂尔干和韦伯代表的传统。

- fragment: 分裂
- Parsons: 帕森斯
- first major social theorist: 第一个主要社会理论家
- provide: 提供
- synthesis: 综合
- classical social theory: 古典社会理论
- traditions: 传统
- represented by: 由...代表
- Marx: 马克思
- Durkheim: 涂尔干
- Weber: 韦伯

4. The rival religious beliefs that fragmented Western Europe quickly accelerated into armed combat. (Fiero, 2011d)

> 使西欧分裂的对立宗教信仰迅速加速为武装战斗。

- fragment: 分裂
- rival religious beliefs: 对立的宗教信仰
- Western Europe: 西欧
- quickly accelerated: 迅速加速
- armed combat: 武装战斗
