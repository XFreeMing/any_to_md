## 推广；宣传

1. Warhol has brilliantly expressed the indifference of the mass media, whose objective is to promote celebrities by saturating a thirsty public with their likenesses but which tells us nothing meaningful about them and shows no concern for them. (Davies et al., 2015)

> 沃霍尔巧妙地表达了大众媒体的冷漠，其目标是通过用名人的形象来满足渴望的公众来推广名人，但它没有告诉我们任何有意义的东西，也没有表现出对他们的关心。

- promote: 推广，宣传
- brilliantly expressed: 巧妙地表达
- indifference: 冷漠
- mass media: 大众媒体
- objective: 目标
- celebrities: 名人
- saturating: 满足
- thirsty public: 渴望的公众
- likenesses: 形象
- meaningful: 有意义的
- concern: 关心

2. They [Post-Impressionists] also maintained the anti-bourgeois, anti-academic attitude of the Impressionists, similarly turning to artists cooperatives and private galleries to promote their art. (Davies et al., 2015)

> 他们[后印象派]也保持了印象派的反资产阶级、反学院派的态度，同样转向艺术家合作社和私人画廊来推广他们的艺术。

- promote: 推广
- maintained: 保持
- anti-bourgeois: 反资产阶级的
- anti-academic: 反学院派的
- attitude: 态度
- Impressionists: 印象派
- similarly: 同样地
- turning to: 转向
- artists cooperatives: 艺术家合作社
- private galleries: 私人画廊
- art: 艺术

3. When the pope finally acted against Luther in 1520, it was too late; Luther had been given the needed time to promote his views. (Perry, 2010)

> 当教皇最终在1520年对路德采取行动时，已经太晚了；路德已经得到了推广他观点所需的时间。

- promote: 推广
- pope: 教皇
- finally: 最终
- acted against: 对...采取行动
- too late: 太晚了
- given: 给予
- needed time: 所需的时间
- views: 观点

4. His [Constantine's] choice to accept and promote Christianity was a turning point in history, as it resulted in the union of Christianity with the legacy of the Roman Empire. (Davies et al., 2015)

> 他[君士坦丁]接受并推广基督教的选择是历史上的一个转折点，因为它导致了基督教与罗马帝国遗产的结合。

- promote: 推广
- choice: 选择
- accept: 接受
- Christianity: 基督教
- turning point: 转折点
- history: 历史
- resulted in: 导致
- union: 结合
- legacy: 遗产
- Roman Empire: 罗马帝国
