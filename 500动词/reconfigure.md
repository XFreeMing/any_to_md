1. Both hardware and software have been totally reconfigured, if the computer is scheduled to resell to others. (Oxford English Dictionary, 1989)

> 如果计算机计划转售给他人，硬件和软件都已完全重新配置。

- reconfigure: 重新配置
- both hardware and software: 硬件和软件
- totally: 完全地
- computer: 计算机
- scheduled to: 计划
- resell to others: 转售给他人

2. Unfortunately, seller A is unlikely to enjoy a large first-mover advantage with contract design innovations. To replicate an improvement in a physical product, competitors need to reconfigure assembly lines. (Zamir & Teichman, The Oxford Handbook of Behavioral Economics and the Law, 2014)

> 不幸的是，卖方A不太可能享受合同设计创新的巨大先发优势。要复制物理产品的改进，竞争对手需要重新配置装配线。

- reconfigure: 重新配置
- unfortunately: 不幸的是
- seller A: 卖方A
- unlikely to enjoy: 不太可能享受
- large first-mover advantage: 巨大先发优势
- contract design innovations: 合同设计创新
- replicate: 复制
- improvement: 改进
- physical product: 物理产品
- competitors: 竞争对手
- assembly lines: 装配线

3. We need to do reconfigure everyday objects, and our lifestyles, as our world downsizes around us. (Longman Dictionary of Contemporary English, 2015)

> 我们需要重新配置日常物品和我们的生活方式，因为我们的世界在我们周围缩小。

- reconfigure: 重新配置
- need to: 需要
- everyday objects: 日常物品
- lifestyles: 生活方式
- as: 因为
- world: 世界
- downsizes: 缩小
- around us: 在我们周围
