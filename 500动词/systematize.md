- just as: 正如

2. Bryan Turner, one theorist who has attempted to systematize a theory of the body, summarizes Foucault's thesis: "The body as an object of power is produced in order to be controlled, identified, and reproduced." (Guinn, 2006)

3. The High Middle Ages also gave birth to a cultural revival that led to a rediscovery of important aspects of the Classical heritage, to new centers of learning in the universities, to the use of reason to systematize the study of theology, to the development of a vernacular literature that appealed to both knights and townspeople, and to a dramatic increase in the number and size of churches. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 中世纪盛期也催生了一场文化复兴，导致重新发现古典遗产的重要方面，大学中新的学习中心，使用理性来系统化神学研究，发展吸引骑士和市民的方言文学，以及教堂数量和规模的急剧增加。

- townspeople: 市民

- size: 规模
- churches: 教堂

4. Natural law, with extensive resources rooted in Aristotle's Nicomachean Ethics and with Stoic modifications, was developed and systematized in the thirteenth century by Thomas Aquinas; his Summa Theologiae (Ia–IIae,QQ. 90–7, see Thomas Aquinas 1996) became the classical canon for much natural law thinking. (Skorupski, 2012)

> 自然法，其广泛的资源植根于亚里士多德的《尼各马可伦理学》并带有斯多葛派的修改，在13世纪由托马斯·阿奎那发展和系统化；他的《神学大全》（Ia–IIae,QQ. 90–7，见托马斯·阿奎那1996）成为许多自然法思想的经典典范。

- Summa Theologiae: 《神学大全》

- natural law thinking: 自然法思想
