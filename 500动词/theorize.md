1. Marx theorized the inevitability of a workers' revolution which would overthrow the capitalist system and usher in a new society in which there would be no classes - no large-scale divisions between rich and poor. He did not mean that all inequalities between individuals would disappear. (Giddens, 2009)

> 马克思理论化了工人革命的必然性，这场革命将推翻资本主义制度并迎来一个新社会，在这个社会中将没有阶级——没有富人和穷人之间的大规模分裂。他的意思并不是个人之间的所有不平等都会消失。

3. As we have seen, one view shared by early sociological thinkers was that traditional religion would become more and more marginal to the modern world. Marx, Durkheim and Weber all theorized that a process of secularization was bound to occur as societies modernized and became more reliant on science and technology to control and explain the social world. (Giddens, 2009)

> 正如我们所看到的，早期社会学思想家共同持有的一个观点是，传统宗教将越来越边缘化于现代世界。马克思、涂尔干和韦伯都理论化，随着社会现代化并越来越依赖科学和技术来控制和解释社会世界，世俗化过程必然会发生。

4. Leonard Berkowitz (1978, 1989) realized that the original theory overstated the frustration-aggression connection, so he revised it. Berkowitz theorized that frustration produces anger, an emotional readiness to aggress. Anger arises when someone who frustrates us could have chosen to act otherwise (Averill, 1983; Weiner, 1981). (Myers, 2012)

> 伦纳德·伯科维茨（1978，1989）意识到原始理论夸大了挫折-攻击联系，所以他修正了它。伯科维茨理论化，挫折产生愤怒，一种攻击的情感准备。当让我们受挫的人本可以选择以其他方式行动时，愤怒就会出现（艾夫里尔，1983；韦纳，1981）。

- Leonard Berkowitz: 伦纳德·伯科维茨

- original theory: 原始理论
- overstated: 夸大

- revised: 修正
- frustration: 挫折
- produces: 产生
- anger: 愤怒
- emotional readiness: 情感准备
- aggress: 攻击
- arises: 出现
- someone: 某人
- frustrates us: 让我们受挫
- could have chosen: 本可以选择
- act otherwise: 以其他方式行动
