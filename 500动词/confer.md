1. A man who lies and makes excuses for himself by saying "not everybody does that," is someone with an uneasy conscience, because the act of lying implies that a universal value is conferred upon the lie. (Fiero, 2011f)

> 一个撒谎并为自己找借口说"不是每个人都那样做"的人，是一个良心不安的人，因为撒谎的行为意味着谎言被赋予了普遍价值。

- confer: 赋予
- man: 人
- lies: 撒谎
- makes excuses: 找借口
- saying: 说
- not everybody does that: 不是每个人都那样做
- uneasy conscience: 良心不安
- act of lying: 撒谎的行为
- implies: 意味着
- universal value: 普遍价值
- upon: 在...上
- lie: 谎言

2. Unlike objects in nature, humans are self-aware beings who confer meaning and purpose on what they do. (Giddens, 2009)

> 与自然界的物体不同，人类是自我意识的存在，他们为自己所做的事情赋予意义和目的。

- confer: 赋予
- unlike: 与...不同
- objects in nature: 自然界的物体
- humans: 人类
- self-aware beings: 自我意识的存在
- meaning: 意义
- purpose: 目的
- what they do: 他们所做的事情

3. To obtain money, reward favorites, and weaken the old nobility, French kings had sold titles of nobility to members of the bourgeoisie and had conferred noble status on certain government offices bought by wealthy bourgeois. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 为了获得金钱、奖励宠臣和削弱旧贵族，法国国王已经将贵族头衔出售给资产阶级成员，并将贵族地位授予由富有的资产阶级购买的某些政府职位。

- confer: 授予
- to obtain: 为了获得
- money: 金钱
- reward: 奖励
- favorites: 宠臣
- weaken: 削弱
- old nobility: 旧贵族
- French kings: 法国国王
- sold: 出售
- titles of nobility: 贵族头衔
- members: 成员
- bourgeoisie: 资产阶级
- noble status: 贵族地位
- certain government offices: 某些政府职位
- bought by: 由...购买
- wealthy bourgeois: 富有的资产阶级

4. Looking back at the artists of the fifteenth century, the artist and art historian Giorgio Vasari wrote in 1550, "Truly great was the advancement conferred on the arts of architecture, painting, and sculpture by those excellent masters." (Davies et al., 2015)

> 回顾15世纪的艺术家，艺术家和艺术史学家乔治·瓦萨里在1550年写道："那些优秀的大师为建筑、绘画和雕塑艺术带来的进步确实是伟大的。"

- looking back at: 回顾
- artists: 艺术家
- fifteenth century: 15世纪
- artist: 艺术家

- truly great: 确实是伟大的
