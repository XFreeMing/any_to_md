1. Cultures have institutionalized ways to relieve guilt: animal and human sacrifices, offerings of grain and money, penitent behavior, confession, denial. In ancient Israel, the sins of the people were periodically laid on a "scapegoat" animal that was then led into the wilderness to carry away the people's guilt. (Myers, 2012)

> 文化已经制度化了缓解内疚的方法：动物和人类牺牲、谷物和金钱的供奉、忏悔行为、告白、否认。在古以色列，人们的罪过定期被放在一只"替罪羊"动物身上，然后被带到荒野中去带走人们的内疚。

2. As two new parties, the Democrats and the Whigs, began to emerge in the late 1820s and early 1830s, they experimented with a variety of mechanisms for unifying their ranks and legitimizing their presidential candidates. In 1832, both held national party conventions for this purpose, and by the 1840s, both were taking steps to institutionalize and regularize this procedure. (Myers, 2012)

> 随着两个新政党，民主党和辉格党，在19世纪20年代末和30年代初开始出现，他们尝试了各种机制来统一他们的队伍并使他们的总统候选人合法化。在1832年，双方都为此目的举行了全国政党大会，到19世纪40年代，双方都在采取措施将这一程序制度化和规范化。

3. Despite its many achievements, Roman civilization presents a paradox. On the one hand, Roman culture and law evidence high standards of civilization.  On the other, the Romans institutionalized barbaric practices: battles to the death between armed gladiators and the tormenting and slaughtering of wild beasts. The major forms of entertainment in both the Republic and the Empire were chariot races, wild- animal shows, and gladiatorial combat. (Perry, 2010)

> 尽管罗马文明取得了许多成就，但它呈现出一个悖论。一方面，罗马文化和法律证明了高度的文明标准。另一方面，罗马人将野蛮的做法制度化：武装角斗士之间的生死搏斗以及对野生动物的折磨和屠杀。共和国和帝国的主要娱乐形式是战车比赛、野生动物表演和角斗。
