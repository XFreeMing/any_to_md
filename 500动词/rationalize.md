3. If prejudice rationalizes discriminatory behavior, we can mandate nondiscrimination. If social institutions support prejudice, we can pull out those supports (for example, with media that model interracial harmony). If outgroups seem more homogeneous than they really are, we can make efforts to personalize their members. If automatic prejudices lead us to engage in behaviors that make us feel guilty, we can use that guilt to motivate ourselves to break the prejudice habit. (Myers, 2012)

> 如果偏见合理化了歧视行为，我们可以强制要求不歧视。如果社会制度支持偏见，我们可以撤除那些支持（例如，通过媒体展示种族间和谐）。如果外群体看起来比实际情况更同质，我们可以努力个性化他们的成员。如果自动偏见导致我们从事让我们感到内疚的行为，我们可以利用这种内疚来激励自己打破偏见习惯。

4. What, then, is the relationship between religion and prejudice? The answer we get depends on how we ask the question. If we define religiousness as church membership or willingness to agree at least superficially with traditional religious beliefs, then the more religious people are the more racially prejudiced. Bigots often rationalize bigotry with religion. (Myers, 2012)

> 那么，宗教和偏见之间的关系是什么？我们得到的答案取决于我们如何提问。如果我们将宗教性定义为教会成员身份或至少表面上同意传统宗教信仰的意愿，那么越虔诚的人就越有种族偏见。偏执者经常用宗教来合理化偏执。
