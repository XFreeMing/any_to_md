1. White men who covet that portion of the human race receive them in a little house they have erected on the shore, where they have entrenched themselves with two pieces of cannon and twenty guards. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 那些觊觎那部分人类的白人男子在海边建造了一座小房子来接收他们，他们在那里用两门大炮和二十名守卫固守阵地。

- entrench: 固守，巩固

- covet: 觊觎

- portion: 部分

- human race: 人类

- receive: 接收

- erected: 建造

- shore: 海边

- cannon: 大炮

- guards: 守卫

  

2. The essence of Modernism lies in the use of the characteristic methods of a discipline to criticize the discipline itself—not in order to subvert it, but to entrench it more firmly in its area of competence. (Kleiner, 2015)

> 现代主义的本质在于使用一个学科的特征方法来批评该学科本身——不是为了颠覆它，而是为了更牢固地巩固它在自己能力范围内的地位。

- entrench: 巩固

- essence: 本质

- Modernism: 现代主义

- lies in: 在于

- characteristic methods: 特征方法

- discipline: 学科

- criticize: 批评

- subvert: 颠覆

- firmly: 牢固地

- area of competence: 能力范围

  

3. At the end of World War II, Soviet military forces remained in all the lands they had liberated from the Nazis in Eastern Europe and the Balkans except for Greece, Albania, and Yugoslavia. Between 1945 and 1947, one-party Communist governments became firmly entrenched in East Germany, Bulgaria, Romania, Poland, and Hungary. (Spielvogel, 2010)

> 第二次世界大战结束时，苏联军队留在他们从纳粹手中解放的东欧和巴尔干地区的所有土地上，除了希腊、阿尔巴尼亚和南斯拉夫。1945年至1947年间，一党制共产党政府在东德、保加利亚、罗马尼亚、波兰和匈牙利牢固地确立。

- entrench: 确立，巩固

- end of World War II: 第二次世界大战结束

- Soviet military forces: 苏联军队

- remained: 留在

- liberated: 解放

- Nazis: 纳粹

- Eastern Europe: 东欧

- Balkans: 巴尔干地区

- except for: 除了

- one-party Communist governments: 一党制共产党政府

- firmly: 牢固地

  

4. Although more women are entering paid work in the UK than ever before, occupational segregation between 'a man's job' and 'women's work' in the labour force remains entrenched.

> 尽管在英国，进入有偿工作的女性比以往任何时候都多，但劳动力中"男性工作"和"女性工作"之间的职业隔离仍然根深蒂固。

- entrench: 根深蒂固

- entering: 进入

- paid work: 有偿工作

- UK: 英国

- occupational segregation: 职业隔离

- labour force: 劳动力

- remains: 仍然
