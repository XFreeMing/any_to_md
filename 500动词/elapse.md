1. Your fame will grow as time elapses. (Fiero, 2011)

> 随着时间流逝，你的名声会增长。

- elapse: 流逝
- fame: 名声
- grow: 增长
- as: 随着
- time: 时间

2. I think for most people, the story about Karen is probably easier to read, and easier to visualize. It's also likely that you could re-tell Karen's story with reasonable accuracy, even after some time has elapsed. (Dirksen, 2011)

> 我认为对大多数人来说，关于卡伦的故事可能更容易阅读，也更容易可视化。即使经过一些时间，你也可能以合理的准确性重新讲述卡伦的故事。

- elapse: 流逝
- think: 认为
- most people: 大多数人
- story: 故事
- Karen: 卡伦
- easier to read: 更容易阅读
- easier to visualize: 更容易可视化
- likely: 可能
- re-tell: 重新讲述
- reasonable accuracy: 合理的准确性
- even after: 即使经过
- some time: 一些时间

3. More than a year elapsed between the first fighting at Lexington and the Declaration of Independence. (Goodwin, 1976)

> 在列克星敦的第一次战斗和《独立宣言》之间，一年多的时间过去了。

- elapse: 流逝
- more than a year: 一年多
- between: 在...之间
- first fighting: 第一次战斗
- Lexington: 列克星敦
- Declaration of Independence: 《独立宣言》

4. "If," said Gibbon, "a man were called upon to fix the period during which the condition of the human race was most happy and prosperous, he would without hesitation name that which elapsed from the accession of Nerva to the death of Marcus Aurelius. (Durant, 2010)

> "如果，"吉本说，"一个人被要求确定人类状况最幸福和繁荣的时期，他会毫不犹豫地指出从涅尔瓦即位到马可·奥勒留去世的那段时期。

- elapse: 流逝
- Gibbon: 吉本
- man: 人
- called upon: 被要求
- fix: 确定
- period: 时期
- condition: 状况
- human race: 人类
- most happy: 最幸福
- prosperous: 繁荣的
- without hesitation: 毫不犹豫地
- name: 指出
- accession: 即位
- Nerva: 涅尔瓦
- death: 去世
- Marcus Aurelius: 马可·奥勒留
