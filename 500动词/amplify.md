1. During actual community conflicts, like-minded people associate increasingly with one another, amplifying their shared tendencies. (Myers, 2012)

  

2. Much aggression is committed by groups. Circumstances that provoke individuals may also provoke groups. By diffusing responsibility and polarizing actions, group situations amplify aggressive reactions. (Myers, 2012)

  

3. Sexual arousal and other forms of arousal, such as anger, can therefore amplify one another (Zillmann, 1989). Love is never so passionate as after a fight or a fright—one reason why it’s so popular to take a hot date to a horror movie. (Myers, 2012)

  

4. Groups can amplify aggressive reactions partly by diffusing responsibility. (Myers, 2012)
